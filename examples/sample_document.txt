Aspirin and ibuprofen are common pain relievers used to treat headaches and fever.
Aspirin was first synthesized in 1897 by a German chemist.
These medications work by inhibiting prostaglandins in the body.
Prostaglandins are hormone-like substances that regulate pain and inflammation.
Over-the-counter pain relievers like acetaminophen can also reduce fever.
However, aspirin has blood-thinning properties that make it useful for preventing heart attacks.
Regular doses of aspirin are recommended for patients at high risk of cardiovascular disease.
The most common side effect of aspirin is gastrointestinal irritation.
Patients with stomach ulcers should avoid taking aspirin regularly.
NSAIDs like ibuprofen have similar anti-inflammatory effects to aspirin.
